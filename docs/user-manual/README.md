## 1. What it is

The pedal of an **electric piano / keyboard** is controlled by detecting the
player's **head angle** with the iOS (or Android) app's AI — sustained ("singing")
notes without using the feet. The bFaaaP Switch system needs **both** the Switch
device and the app. The Switch device (**Japan radio cert. 技適 R018‑180280**) is
**build‑to‑order** — buy it from <https://ui.saaipf.com/app3/> first, then buy the
app.

## 2. Connect the device to the electric piano

1. Check the device cable end — a **6.3 mm 2‑pole (TS) phone plug**.
2. Check the piano's **sustain pedal connector** (6.3 mm 2‑pole).
3. Plug the cable into the **SUSTAIN** jack. *(A 3‑pedal "PEDAL UNIT" is wired
   differently — take care.)*

## 3. Power on device + app

- Press the **button on top of the device** → it **blinks blue**.
- Open the iOS app.

## 4. Select the BLE channel

- A channel button is grey until connectable; when it **turns blue**, tap it.

## 5. Start

- The **first time**, nod your head up/down so the screen toggles **white ↔ red**
  — this hands the angle detection to the device. *(The very first cycle does not
  actuate — expected.)*

## 6. On‑type / Off‑type

- Different electric pianos invert the sustain logic (head‑down → red → sustain
  may be reversed); it can also auto‑switch initially. Use the **On/Off button**
  to flip it if needed.

## 7. Head‑angle threshold

- Hold your head at your chosen neutral position and press **Angle Set** to fix
  the threshold.
- Below the threshold: screen **grey**, device LED **green**.
- Above the threshold: screen **red**, device LED **red**, and the note sustains.

## 8. Support page

The Support button links to this manual, **health & safety notes**, the

## 9. Change BLE channel

- To run **two or more** bFaaaP Switch units, change the channel. Pressing
  **Reset** changes the channel and re‑initializes the device.
