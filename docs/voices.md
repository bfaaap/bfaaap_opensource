# Voices — messages from the bFaaaP team

> 🌐 **English** · [日本語](../i18n/ja/docs/voices.md) · [Deutsch](../i18n/de/docs/voices.md)

When bFaaaP became open source, the people who built it, advised it, and play with it sent a few
words — in their own voice. Here they are, beginning with our founder. Each entry links to that member’s **full message** — their
original words with English & German translations — under [`voices/`](voices/). *(More voices will be added as
they arrive.)*

### 🎹 Tomo Shishido — *founder & project lead*

<sub>📖 **[Read the full message](voices/shishido.md)** — original Japanese + English & German</sub>

<img src="members/shishido-tomoyuki.jpg" alt="Tomo Shishido" width="150" align="left">

Welcome — and thank you for being here. I started the piano at **45**, unable to read a single note,
at the nudge of my late wife: *“You don’t have any friends, so learn the piano for your old age.”*
The lessons were a joy — but I kept thinking how much more I could express **if only I could use the
pedals.** That wish became bFaaaP.

With friends — an architect, a former weather‑satellite engineer, a patent colleague, a tuner,
students, teachers, and a composer — what began as one person’s small wish to **sustain a note** grew
into a shared dream: *a world where everyone can enjoy music more inclusively.* The daily “small
happiness” of the piano’s sound ringing on quietly became a “big happiness” I never expected to find.

Open‑sourcing bFaaaP is our way of passing that on. Please meet **[the people who made it](members/)**
— and if it helps you, or someone you know, **build it, improve it, and play.**

<br clear="left">

— **Tomo Shishido** · founder

### 🛠️ Hiroyuki Narusawa — *pedal device & firmware*

<sub>📖 **[Read the full message](voices/narusawa.md)** — original Japanese + English & German</sub>

<img src="members/narusawa-hiroyuki.jpg" alt="Hiroyuki Narusawa" width="150" align="left">

Toward the open‑source launch: at first this project seemed simple, but once we started, all sorts of
factors became entangled — and even now it isn’t truly finished. By opening it up, I hope many people
get to know it and **use it as a stepping stone**, so that improvements, cost reductions, and new
approaches keep emerging.

Put simply, it comes down to **three elements**:
1. how to **sense** body movement other than the feet, to press the pedal;
2. the means to **send** that data to the device quickly;
3. using that data to **press** the pedal quickly and quietly.

<br clear="left">

— **Hiroyuki Narusawa** · Naru Science Soft

### 🎶 Keiko Nagasawa — *piano instructor · studio PASTEL*

<sub>📖 **[Read the full message](voices/nagasawa.md)** — original Japanese + English & German</sub>

<img src="members/nagasawa-keiko.png" alt="Keiko Nagasawa" width="150" align="left">

Congratulations on the open‑source release. The **pedal** is a vital function that greatly shapes the
expression of piano playing — yet there are people for whom operating it is difficult for physical
reasons.

bFaaaP **removes that barrier** and gives form to the player’s wish to *“play”* and to *“express
more.”* Because it responds even to **delicate pedal changes**, I believe it will help many people
achieve a performance that is *truly their own.* Toward a society where everyone can enjoy music and
express themselves in their own way, I hope this project reaches even more people.

<br clear="left">

— **Keiko Nagasawa** · piano studio **PASTEL** — <https://www.pastelpiano.org>

### 🔧 kana — *piano tuner*

<sub>📖 **[Read the full message](voices/kana.md)** — original Japanese + English & German</sub>

<img src="members/kana.png" alt="kana" width="150" align="left">

Pedalling is **very delicate**: pressing too far can muddy a phrase and lose the beauty of the
harmony. bFaaaP renders that fine control **not with the toes, but through how you tilt your head and
how fast** — letting the player express their music more richly and precisely.

The piano is an instrument anyone in the world can enjoy. As a **tuner**, I’m deeply impressed that
bFaaaP makes such a wide range of playing possible **without placing any burden on the piano’s
action.** With this release, I dearly hope many more people come to know bFaaaP — and that it reaches
those who feel some inconvenience.

<br clear="left">

— **kana**
